Experiments in Gen AI Code Performance Testing

Method

So, I think an interesting level of complication has been added to this project. Currently, I have to specify the coordinates of the outer shape of every letter in the English alphabet, which I am finding rather tiresome to figure out. It took me until the letter J to think:

sod this, isn't this exactly what this AI thing everyone's been talking about for? Not having to figure things out for myself?

and I set about trying different models to solve the problem for me. Luckily, I was able to steal credentials for OpenAI Pro and Grok from a suspecting victim, so I started with comparing two.

First, I required a minor refactor (I said for the upteenth time), so that the series of points defining each letter could be read in as a constant from a file. Then that file could be changed relatively easily, for easy comparison of the output of different models. The prompt I gave to both of them was:

Generate the points to define the geometric shapes of the letters A-Z in the English alphabet, capitalized. Here are some styles guides:

  • Lines should be 1/6 in width
  • You can define curves with this function:
def arc_points(radius, start_angle, end_angle, center=[0,0], num_points=50): 
    angles = [start_angle + i * (end_angle - start_angle) / (num_points - 1)
              for i in range(num_points)]
    points = [
        (center[0] + radius * math.cos(math.radians(angle)),
         center[1] + radius * math.sin(math.radians(angle)))
        for angle in angles
    ]
    return points
  • In order to make solid arcs with width 1/6, have the outer arc with radius = 1/2 and the inner arc with radius 1/3
  • Letters should fill a square where possible.

Here are some examples of letters I've already made, from A-J:

import math

    other_points_B = [(0,0), (0,1)]
    B = top_arc_points_B + bottom_arc_points_B + other_points_B

etc. for each letter (manually defined letters A-J are defined in letters.py).

Analysis

Well, no real points for getting the ones I gave you - oh ye gods o1, how'd you manage to mess up my G? I spent ages figuring out where the bar would intersect with the inner arc. -10 points for that, so not off to a good start. K should be where things get actually interesting, as that's where it's generating instead of just (erroneously!) copy-pasting my coordinates into a different format.

K

O1 pretty good, not a fan of the overly wide lower leg but that's ultimately is superficial, I suppose. Grok, What can I say but you tried... I might very generously call the nubbin on the end of the lower leg a serif, but in my heart of hearts I know that's not true.
O1: 9/10, Grok: 4/10

L

Hard to mess up, honestly. But full marks to everyone, since we're not grading on a curve. One think that does comes to mind is if the order of the points differs between them. Groks is: 
    
      # Grok
      L = [(0,0), (0,1), (1/6,1), (1/6,1/6), (1,1/6), (1,0)]
      # o1
      L = [(0,0), (1,0), (1,1/6), (1/6,1/6), (1/6,1), (0,1) ]
which cycle counterclockwise and clockwise around the letter, respectively.
O1: 10/10, Grok: 10/10

M

Is it weird I actually like the Grock one more? The o1 is more technically correct, with it's only real fault being that the divot down is lower than it should be. Grok's M is, as with it's K, perhaps permissible under the generous label of "font" but... no, it's not right. However, to my eye it seems to reflect the more fundamental properties of the M, the middle divot starting at the top and going all the way to the bottom.
O1: 9/10, Grok: 6/10

N

Aaaaaand you failed! This is when not grading on a curve comes back to bite you. Only differece of note is that o1 makes the lower right corner along a ridge, whereas Grok's sharpens to a point. Both of them seem to have most of the right points, but in different order than they should be.
O1: 2/10, Grok: 2/10

O

Kind of an L situation, I'm not terribly impressed by it is rather nice that it works. Full marks for everyone! O1: 10/10, Grok: 10/10

P

Here, o1's and Grok's results are two halves of the whole, with o1 missing two points that Grok has, and Grok missing the half of the arc that o1 has. Interesting, makes me wonder about the meta parameters of Grok and if it was specifically designed to fill the gaps of OpenAI's models
O1: 7/10, Grok 6/10

Q

O1's here is pretty poor attempt. I'm fairly impressed by Grok's Q, I think the positioning of the bar looks stylish and balanced. Obvious flaw of the weird hole, which seems to be similar type of defect of the o1's result.
O1: 3/10, Grok: 7/10

R

Again, here it seems to me like o1 and Grok have focused on separate parts of the letter, with o1 getting details right but the overall shape wrong, and o1 focussing on the overall shape but missing important details. On the Grok result, again we see the return of the weird serif found on K.
O1: 4/10, Grok: 3/10

S

Big win for o1 here, the S is not bad (if backwards). Grok has completely lost the plot on this one. I think that's because here, o1 had to violate the 1/6 width rule to achieve the shape, whereas Grok tried to follow the guidelines more closely but wasn't able to.
O1: 6/10, Grok: 3/10

T

Here's another one that o1 should have knocked out the park like Grok, but didn't follow the guidelines and made the vertical line far too wide. Maybe Grok follows the user prompt more closely, even if that means it sometimes fails. In contrast, I get the impression that o1 is more willing to deviate from my initial ask to try and give me what it thinks I want, either having a higher temperature or some more initial prompting I'm not privy to.
O1: 7/10, Grok: 10/10

U

So, here they both invert the curve of the U to point upwards instead of downwards. However, Grok actually has the lines on either side of the curve that I want, whereas o1 has the inverse of those lines. Ultimately, Grok's is closer to want I'd want.
O1: 3/10, Grok: 5/10
